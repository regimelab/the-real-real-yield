The bond market’s inflation forecasts are **most accurate for 1–2 years out**, but there is a well-documented **cliff around 5–10 years** where the market becomes habitually unreliable.

### Accuracy by time horizon

| Horizon | Forecast accuracy | What the data shows |
|---|---|---|
| **0–1 year** | **Modestly accurate** | Breakeven inflation from TIPS has slightly better predictive power than economists over this range  [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists) |
| **1–2 years** | **Best performance** | Bond traders outperform economists for predicting inflation over 1–2 years  [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists) |
| **3–5 years** | **Rapidly degrading** | Accuracy drops sharply; market often misses turning points and persistent inflation  [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists) |
| **5–10+ years** | **Very poor / unreliable** | **Long-term breakeven inflation is not a good predictor** of future inflation; regressions show no meaningful relationship  [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists) |
| **10+ years** | **Economists > bond market** | For 10-year-ahead inflation, professional economists actually forecast better than bond yields  [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists) |

### The “cliff” you’re asking about

The **cliff** occurs around **5 years**:

- **Before ~3–5 years**: market-based inflation expectations (from TIPS breakevens) have some predictive value. [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists)
- **After ~5 years**: long-term inflation compensation derived from bond yields **fails to predict future inflation** and “misses most of the fluctuations” in actual long-term inflation outcomes. [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/who-are-better-forecasters-inflation-bond-traders-or-economists)
- Studies explicitly conclude: **“long-term bond yields and long-term inflation compensation derived from bond yields are not good predictors of future inflation”**. [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/bond-yields-are-not-good-predictors-inflation)

In other words, the market is reasonably good at forecasting inflation **up to about 1–2 years**, starts to lose reliability around **3–5 years**, and becomes **habitually wrong** for **5–10+ year** horizons due to the length of the time frame. [piie](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2021/bond-yields-are-not-good-predictors-inflation)
